Jonathan Joestar

About Jonathan

Jonathan Joestar is the first protagonist of Jojo's Bizarre Adventure. Born to a wealthy british family in Victorian England, Jonathan was raised to be an upstanding and chivalrous gentleman.

His whole life had changed once his adoptive brother, Dio Brando, entered the picture. Dio was motivated to ruin Jonathan's life in any way he could; killing Jonathan's dog and abusing his girlfriend, there was no lengths Dio wouldn't go to assert his superiority.

Upon reaching adulthood, Jonathan's life was once again uprooted and destroyed when he found out Dio had been poisoning Jonathan's father in an attempt to steal the Joestar fortune. After getting discovered, Dio proceeded to murder Jonathan's father and use his blood on an ancient stone mask to transform into a vampire.

Jonathan battled Dio in their childhood home and ended in a draw, with the Joestar manor burning to the ground. After healing from his injuries, Jonathan met William Zepelli, a strange man hunting for the stone mask and Dio. Zepelli took Jonathan under his wing and trained him in the ways of Hamon to prepare for the final battle against Dio. Jonathan has fought many difficult opponents and overcame every obstacle in his path to Dio.

Jonathan had succeeded in defeating Dio and eventually married his childhood sweetheart Erina Pendleton.

While on their honeymoon, Jonathan discovered Dio had survived their battle and stowed away on their cruise ship. Dio sought to steal Jonathan's body due to Dio being reduced to only his head. Unfortunately, Dio managed to fatally wound Jonathan and Jonathan succumbed to his injuries and died trying to protect Erina and remaining survivors on the cruise ship.

Joseph Joestar

About Joseph

Joseph Joestar is the second protagonist of the series. Joseph is the grandson of Jonathan Joestar and was raised primarily by his grandmother, Erina. Joseph is a delinquent and often gets himself into trouble. Although never receiving formal training, Joseph is able to use Hamon much like Jonathan. Despite his hoodlum tendencies, Joseph is cunning, battle-smart, and cares deeply about his loved ones and would go to great lengths to protect them.

Joseph's story entailed teaming up with the grandson of William Zepelli, Caesar, and his father figure, Robert E.O Speedwagon to battle the Pillar Men, an ancient race of human-like creatures that are seeking the Red Stone of Aja. Joseph had challenged the Pillar Men to a duel in three months in an fit of rage. The Pillar Men planted poisonous rings within Joseph that will kill him after the three months have passed. Joseph and Caesar sought out Lisa Lisa, Ceasar's mentor, to train their Hamon skills to prepare for the fight while protecting the Red Stone of Aja. Joseph was able to successfully defeat on of the Pillar Men, Esidisi, in an ambush. Unfortunately, Caesar lost his life in a revenge-fueled battle against the second Pillar Man, Wamuu and passed his will onto Joseph, along with the antidote for the poison rings.

Joseph was able to defeat Wamuu and avenged Caesar in a gladiator-style battle. The last of the Pillar Men, Kars, had successfully stolen the Red Stone of Aja and used it's power along with the stone mask to not only become immune to sunlight, but also became the ultimate life form. Joseph tried everything he could think of to defeat Kars, but Kars proved to be invincible. In a shocking twist, Joseph had able to defeat Kars by using the red stone to activate a volcanic eruption, sending the two towards the edge of space. Kars drifted too far outside the atmosphere and got pulled into the vacuum of space, where he drifted aimlessly for eternity. Joseph had survived the fall and reunited with his loved ones at his own funeral, revealing that he survived and got married to Suzie Q, Lisa Lisa's assistant. Joseph lived out the rest of his years in peace and had a daughter name Holly.

Jotaro Kujo

About Jotaro

Jotaro Kujo is the third protagonist of the series. Jotaro is the grandson of Joseph and is half-Japanese. Jotaro is stoic, analytical, and has a nasty temper when it comes to authority and women. Also a high school delinquent, Jotaro often gets himself into trouble; From getting into fights with arrogant teachers, and not paying for his meal at restaurants if he's dissatisfied, Jotaro is the quintessential bad boy.

Unlike Joseph and Jonathan, Jotaro doesn't use Hamon. Instead he, along with Joseph, have obtained Stands; a physical manifestation of one's fighting spirit. Joseph reveals to Jotaro that Dio had survived and had stolen Jonathan's body as his own, running amok somewhere in the world. Joseph also revealed that they manifested Stands due to a psychic link between them and Jonathan's body, meaning that Dio had also possessed a Stand. Jotaro's mother, Holly, also developed a Stand but was unable to withstand the curse Dio placed on the Joestars and was on the verge of death.

Jotaro, using his stand Star Platinum, was able to pinpoint Dio's location in Egypt, so Jotaro, along with Joseph, Avdol, Kakyoin and newcomers Polnareff and Iggy, made their way to Egypt to defeat Dio and save Holly. They battled many of Dio's minions on their way to Egypt, taking many detours, but have successfully made it to Egypt and found Dio's hideout.

The remaining minions of Dio have battled them and the group were barely able to defeat them at the cost of Avdol and Iggy's lives. The remaining group battled Dio throughout Cairo whilst trying to discover Dio's power. It was revealed by Kakyoin, after being slain by Dio, that Dio's Stand has the ability to stop time. Jotaro, also revealed to have the ability to stop time, managed to kill Dio and was able to revive Joseph after Dio had killed him and drained his blood to strengthen his vampiric abilities.

Josuke Higashikata

About Josuke

Josuke Higashikata is our fourth protagonist in the series. He's the illegitimate son of Joseph and Jotaro's uncle, despite being a high school student. While he has the facade of a typical Japanese delinquent, Josuke has a heart of gold but will snap immediately if you insult his hair. Also a Stand user, Josuke's Stand is called Crazy Diamond and has the ability to heal other people's injuries but can't use it to heal himself. He cares deeply about his friends and family and would put his life on the line to protect them. Josuke's story takes place in the town of Morioh, where a serial killer named Yoshikage Kira is running amok and murdering innocent people. Josuke, along with Jotaro and Joseph, team up with other stand users living within Morioh to locate Kira and stop him once and for all.

Josuke and his friends have gone through many challenges to locate Kira, even losing some of their allies in the process. They were able to locate Kira, but he unfortunately escaped their grasp and has dawned a new identity along with a new ability that allows him to turn back time.

Josuke and his friends were able to identify Kira and plan to meet Hayato, a boy whose father was killed by Kira and took on his persona. By sheer coincidence, Hayato ran into Josuke's group, with Kira tailing the boy. After battling both Kira and Kira's father, Josuke and his group were able to severely wound Kira and in ironic fashion, Kira was killed due do an ambulence running him over whilst backing up.

Giorno Giovanna

About Giorno

Giorno Giovanna is the fifth protagonist of the series. Giorno, like other members of the Joestar bloodline, also possesses a Stand called Gold Experience which can heal injuries and give life to inanimate objects; it can even be used to regenerate body parts.

Born as the illegitimate son of Dio, and by extension Jonathan, Giorno had a very difficult childhood. He had a neglectful mother and an abusive step father, along with constantly getting picked on. Giorno's life changed forever when he saved a fatally injured man using his Stand Gold Experience. This man turned out to be a member of the Italian Mafia, and the man thanked Giorno by protecting him from his step father's abuse and from the kids who picked on him. At 15 years old, Giorno met a man named Bruno Bucciarati, who is also a member of the mafia. Giorno expressed to Bucciarati his disdain towards the mafia selling drugs to the children of Italy, and he wanted to take down the boss to make a change in their world.

Giorno teamed up with Bruno along with the members of Bruno's syndicate: Abbachio, Narancia, Mista and Fugo. They also forged an alliance with Trish, the supposed daughter of the boss and was placed under their protection on the Boss's behalf. Together they fought to climb up the Mafia ranks to get closer to discovering the boss's true identity. They battled many of the rogue members of the mafia, many of whom were sent by the boss to stop them. Eventually, they learned at the boss's true identity: a man named Diavolo, who suffers from Multiple Personality Disorder, and his Stand ability: Time Erasure. They also learned about the boss's real motive, to kill Trish to protect his identity and to retreive the Stand Arrow, which is how many characters have Stands to begin with.

After losing Abbachio, Narancia, and Bucciarati along with Fugo deserting the group, Giorno and the remainder of the group had successfully defeated the boss thanks to Giorno receiving an upgrade to his Stand courtesy of the arrow. Giorno, using his new Stand ability, sent the boss in an infinite death loop, never to find out what happened. Giorno went on to become the new mafia boss and reform the syndicate.

Jolyne Cujoh

About Jolyne

Jolyne is our sixth protagonis of the series and our final member of the Joestar family prior to the series reboot. Jolyne is the daughter of Jotaro Kujo and is much like her father; an ill-tempered delinquent constantly getting into trouble. Jolyne has distain towards Jotaro due to him being absent from her life and holds nothing but resentment for him.

Jolyne's story begins with her being framed for murder and thrown into prison. Jolyne also obtained her own Stand thanks to a pendant from Jotaro which contained a fragment of a Stand Arrow called Stone Ocean. Jolyne did her best to survive prison, gaining allies when she could, but she was surprised to see Jotaro came to visit her in an attempt so help her escape. Jolyne hesitantly went with Jotaro and just as they were about to make it out of the prison, Jotaro was attacked by a Stand named Pale Snake, who possessed the ability to remove a person's memories and Stand ability via discs. Left in suspended animation, Jotaro was left to the care of the Speedwagon Foundation, a multi-conglomerate founded by Robert Speedwagon to aid the Joestar family.

With newfound resolve, Jolyne went back to prison to retreive her father's memory and Stand discs and to find out the identity of Pale Snake. Jolyne along with her friends Ermes, F.F., Anasui, Weather Forecast, and Emporio have found out who Pale Snale really is: a preist in the prison named Pucci. Pucci was Dio's best friend and successor, and his motivation was to enact Dio's dream of reaching Heaven; a world where everyone knew their fate and can make peace with the inevitable.

Jolyne and co. did their best to stop Pucci from resetting the universe but have all lost their lives trying to protect Emporio. In the new universe, Emporio was able to defeat Pucci using Weather Forecast's Stand and brought the universe back to the way it was, with Jolyne and everyone coming back to life as new people with happier lives.

Dio Brando

About Dio

Dio is the overarching antagonist of the series and is the lifelong enemy of the Joestar lineage. Born in the slums of London, Dio had a hellish upbringing; severely abused by his father, and playing chess competitively to have enough money to feed himself. Dio felt like an underdog throughout his life and that he needs to fight tooth and nail to get to the top of society. His golden opportunity came when his father handed him a letter from his father that is addressed to the Joestar family. Dio's father told him to go to them upon his death for a better life. Dio was adopted into the Joestar family and immediately proceeded to make Jonathan's life a living hell. Embarassing him in a boxing match, spreading rumors, even killing Jonathan's dog, there's no line Dio wouldn't cross to break Jonathan's spirit. Once Jonathan fought back, Dio decided to be more sublte with his actions. Upon adulthood, Dio developed a comradere with Jonathan, which was only a facade. Dio's true motivation was to steal the Joestar family fortune for himself by poisoning Jonathan's father George and murdering Jonathan afterwards. Jonathan caught wind of his plans and started investigating, forcing Dio to run off with the stone mask. Dio learned that the mask turns the user into a vampire using blood and saved it for a backup plan. Upon returning to the Joestar manor, Jonathan, his new friend Speedwagon, and 6 policemen were there to meet Dio. backed into a corner, Dio stabbed George and used his blood to activate the stone mask, transforming into a vampire and slaying everyone within the manor. Jonathan and Dio's battle ended in a stalemate, and Dio survived the burning of the Manor and took refuge in a village, turning everyone into zombies and consuming many souls to regenerate. Dio's second battle with Jonathan ended with Dio cutting off his own head in a long fall. Dio then stowed himself away on Jonathan and Erina's cruise ship, turned evryone on the ship into zombies, and fatally wounded Jonathan to steal his body.

Dio survived the destruction of the ship and slept in a coffin at the bottom of the Atlantic ocean for 100 years. Upon reakwakening, Dio was a new man; more philisophical and inquisitive about life and his own psychological nature. He met a young man named Pucci and the two quickly became friends, discussing many topics about philisoph, art and more. This was where Dio impressed upon Pucci the idea of reaching Heaven, a state of enlightenment where everyone knows their fate and can make peace with it, freeing themselves from desitny's uncertainty.
In the year 1988, Dio declared war on the Joestar family once again and had newfound motivation to erase them from this world; he sent many assassins and minions after Jotaro's group to prevent them from reaching Dio, but Jotaro and his group defeated them all. Once the group reached Cairo, Dio stopped at nothing to kill them using his Stand, The World, which has the ability to stop time. Dio used his power to kill both Kakyoin and Joseph, along with wounding Polnareff. Dio and Jotaro proceeded to fight throughout Cairo, but eventually Jotaro got the upper hand even after Dio consumed Joseph's blood to enhance his powers. Dio was killed by Jotaro and his body disintergrated upon sunrise.
